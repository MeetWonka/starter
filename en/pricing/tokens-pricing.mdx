## What Are Tokens?

<Info>
Tokens are the units that measure AI model usage. Both your input (prompt) and the AI's response (completion) consume tokens. Think of tokens as roughly 3/4 of a word in English.

Example: "Hello, how are you today?" ≈ 6 tokens
</Info>

All token prices are listed in USD per 1 million tokens.

## Choosing the Right Model

<Steps>
<Step title="Define Your Use Case">

**Simple tasks** (FAQ, categorization, summaries):
- Use budget models: DeepSeek ($0.14), Ministral ($0.04-0.10), Gemini Flash ($0.10)

**General business tasks** (emails, reports, research):
- Use balanced models: GPT-4o Mini ($0.15), Claude Haiku ($0.80), Gemini Pro ($1.25)

**Complex reasoning** (analysis, strategy, coding):
- Use advanced models: GPT-4o ($2.50), Claude Sonnet ($3.00), O1-Mini ($1.10)

**Maximum capability** (critical decisions, research):
- Use premium models: Claude Opus ($15.00), O1 ($15.00), GPT-4 Turbo ($10.00)

</Step>

<Step title="Consider Volume & Cost">

**High-volume operations** (chatbots, automation):
- Prioritize cost-per-token: Amazon Nova Micro ($0.035), Ministral 3B ($0.04), DeepSeek ($0.14)

**Medium volume**:
- Balance cost & quality: Gemini 2.0 Flash ($0.10), GPT-4o Mini ($0.15), Claude Haiku ($0.80)

**Low volume, high stakes**:
- Prioritize quality: Claude Opus ($15.00), O1 ($15.00), GPT-4 Turbo ($10.00)

</Step>

<Step title="Test & Optimize">

Start with a mid-tier model, measure quality, then adjust:
- **Quality too low?** → Upgrade to more capable model
- **Quality acceptable?** → Test cheaper alternatives
- **Repeated prompts?** → Use Claude with caching for 90% savings

</Step>
</Steps>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="How are tokens counted?" icon="calculator">
Tokens are counted for both input (your prompt + agent instructions) and output (the AI's response).

Rough estimate: 1 token ≈ 0.75 English words

Example conversation:
- Your question: "Summarize this document" (3 tokens)
- Document content: 2,000 words (≈2,666 tokens)
- AI summary: 200 words (≈267 tokens)
- Total: ~2,936 tokens consumed
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Which model should I use?" icon="circle-question">
Start here:
- Most teams: GPT-4o Mini (0.15 USD) - best value
- Budget-conscious: DeepSeek (0.14 USD) or Gemini Flash (0.10 USD)
- Complex tasks: Claude Sonnet 4 (3.00 USD) or GPT-4o (2.50 USD)
- Reasoning tasks: O1-Mini (1.10 USD) for chain-of-thought

Test with your actual use case to find the sweet spot.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I switch models?" icon="arrows-rotate">
Yes! You can configure different models for different agents. Use expensive models only where quality matters most.

Strategy: 
- Customer-facing: Premium models
- Internal tools: Budget models
- Testing: Free/cheap models
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is prompt caching?" icon="hard-drive">
Available on Claude models only. If you send similar prompts repeatedly (e.g., same system instructions), Claude caches the repeated parts.

First request: Pay full "write" price (3.75 USD per 1M for Sonnet)  
Subsequent requests: Pay 10× cheaper "read" price (0.30 USD per 1M)

Best for: Agents with long system prompts, repeated document analysis
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Are there free models?" icon="gift">
Some models offer free tiers:
- Gemma series (Google): Free for experimentation
- Gemini 2.0/2.5: Free for limited time

However, production use typically requires paid tokens.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How is token pricing calculated?" icon="coins">
Token pricing is based on:
1. Input tokens: Your prompt + system instructions + context
2. Output tokens: The AI's generated response

Different models have different pricing for input vs output. Most models charge less for input than output.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
